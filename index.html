A legacy of craftsmanship

Our Story

Founded by Donald Herbin, Southeastern Lapidary Associates has been transforming raw stones into exquisite works of art for over three decades. Based in Greensboro, North Carolina, we specialize in revealing the hidden beauty within rough material, crafting custom cabochons, faceted gems, and polished specimens. Our commitment to quality, fair pricing, and preserving the lapidary arts sets us apart in the gemstone community.

NC Native Stones

We specialize in stones native to North Carolina, including hiddenite, emeralds, and rubies.

Master Craftsmanship

Decades of experience in cutting, shaping, and polishing precious and semi-precious stones.

Fair Prices

Direct from the lapidary, offering high-quality stones at competitive and honest prices.

Local Treasures

North Carolina Gems

Discover the treasures hidden in our own backyard

Ruby

Found in the Cowee Valley, these stunning red corundum stones are a state treasure.

Sapphire

Available in various colors, North Carolina sapphires are prized for their clarity.

Emerald

Hiddenite and Spruce Pine are famous for these brilliant green beryl crystals.

Garnet

Deep red almandine and rhodolite garnets are commonly found across the state.

Aquamarine

Beautiful blue beryl crystals found in the pegmatites of the Blue Ridge Mountains.

Smoky Quartz

A popular and abundant crystal featuring varying shades of translucent brown.

How It Works

Our Process

How we turn rough rocks into refined gems

Step 1
Selection
Choosing the right rough material based on color, clarity, and structural integrity.
Step 2
Slabbing
Using a diamond saw to cut the rough rock into workable slices.
Step 3
Preforming
Roughly shaping the stone on a coarse grinding wheel.
Step 4
Smoothing
Refining the shape and removing scratches with progressively finer grits.
Step 5
Polishing
The final step to achieve a brilliant, mirror-like finish.
