01 Executive Summary

Southeastern Lapidary Associates (SLA) will operate as a DBA under Donald Herbin's existing landscaping LLC, providing professional lapidary services — the art and science of cutting, shaping, polishing, and finishing gemstones and ornamental stones. Based in Greensboro, NC, the business will capitalize on North Carolina's rich gemological heritage (the state is home to rubies, sapphires, emeralds, and garnets) and the growing U.S. market for colored gemstones.

08 Vietnam Sourcing Strategy (Phase 3)

8.1 Why Vietnam?

Vietnam is a world-class source for premium gemstones, particularly from the Luc Yen and Quy Chau mining districts. Direct sourcing eliminates multiple middlemen and can reduce rough material costs by 40–60% compared to U.S. wholesale prices.

8.2 Luc Yen Gem Market

The Luc Yen gem market in Yen Bai Province is a professional, early-morning trading hub:

  • Hours: 6:30 AM – Late Morning
  • Sellers: Local miners and established traders
  • Products: Rough and cut ruby, sapphire, spinel
  • Culture: Professional but informal; trust-based relationships are critical
  • Key Success Factor: A reliable local contact/fixer who knows the miners and can verify quality

8.3 Import Logistics & Regulations

Trade regulations are fluid as of 2026. Always consult a licensed U.S. customs broker before importing. The information below is a general guide.

U.S. Import Requirements

RequirementDetail
Formal Customs EntryRequired for shipments valued ≥ $2,500
Customs BondCBP Form 301 required for formal entries
HTS ClassificationChapter 71 — Precious/semi-precious stones
DocumentationCommercial Invoice, Packing List, Bill of Lading/Air Waybill
Country of OriginMust document origin for tariff classification
Current Tariff~10% global tariff (temporary, subject to change)
Historical StatusLoose, unmounted precious stones historically duty-free

Import Checklist

  • Register as an importer with U.S. Customs & Border Protection (CBP)
  • Obtain an Importer of Record number
  • Establish relationship with a licensed customs broker
  • Research current HTS codes for specific stone types
  • Set up documentation templates (commercial invoices)
  • Open a customs bond if shipping > $2,500 per shipment
  • Verify current tariff rates (check AGTA and CBP updates)

Stone Cost = (Rough Material Cost / Expected Yield) + (Machine Time × Hourly Rate) + (Blade/Consumable Wear per Stone) + Overhead Allocation Retail Price = Stone Cost × 2.5–4.0 (markup) Wholesale Price = Stone Cost × 1.5–2.0 (markup)
