1. Executive Summary

Southeastern Lapidary Associates (SLA) will operate as a DBA under Donald Herbin's existing landscaping LLC, providing professional lapidary services — the art and science of cutting, shaping, polishing, and finishing gemstones and ornamental stones. Based in Greensboro, NC, the business will capitalize on North Carolina's rich gemological heritage (the state is home to rubies, sapphires, emeralds, and garnets) and the growing U.S. market for colored gemstones.

Business Model Summary

SLA will generate revenue through five primary channels:

  1. Custom Stone Cutting & Polishing — B2B and B2C services for jewelers, collectors, and hobbyists
  2. Finished Stone Sales — Cabochons, faceted stones, and tumbled stones sold online and at gem shows
  3. Rough Stone Sales — NC-sourced and eventually Vietnam-sourced rough material
  4. Workshops & Classes — Hands-on lapidary instruction for hobbyists
  5. Wholesale to Jewelers — Volume supply relationships with independent jewelry makers

2. Industry Analysis & Market Research

2.1 The Lapidary Industry

The lapidary industry encompasses the cutting, shaping, polishing, and engraving of stones — both precious gemstones and semi-precious ornamental materials. It sits at the intersection of artisanal craftsmanship, geology, and the jewelry supply chain.

Industry Trends Favoring SLA

  1. Premiumization — Consumers buying fewer, higher-quality pieces → favors custom/artisan work over mass production
  2. Colored Stone Renaissance — Shift from diamond-centric to colored engagement rings (sapphire, emerald, morganite)
  3. Origin Story Marketing — Buyers increasingly value provenance (NC-mined, ethically sourced)
  4. Maker Economy — Growing community of independent jewelers seeking reliable stone suppliers
  5. Metaphysical/Wellness Market — Crystals and healing stones represent a multi-billion-dollar niche

11. Operations Plan

Workshop Setup Requirements

  • Water Supply & Drainage: Continuous flow for cooling saws, settling tank for slurry runoff.
  • Electrical: 20A dedicated circuits.
  • Ventilation: Proper dust management (silica dust is a serious hazard).
  • Lighting: LED daylight-balanced for accurate stone grading.
